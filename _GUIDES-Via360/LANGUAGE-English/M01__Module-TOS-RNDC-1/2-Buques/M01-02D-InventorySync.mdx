How cargo inventory records are automatically created when trucks complete weighing, and how to view, filter, and manage them.

## Feature Overview

When a truck completes its final weighing (Estado 8), the system automatically creates a "Carga adicionada" record in the Buques inventory table. Each record is linked back to the originating truck transaction, providing full traceability from truck to inventory.

Operators and administrators can also manually create dispatch entries ("Buque despachado") to record cargo loaded onto ships.

<Note>Only **Admin** and **Operator** roles can create manual dispatch entries. Clients can view inventory records but cannot create them.</Note>

---

## How Automatic Inventory Sync Works

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Step 1 — Truck Completes Final Weighing (Step 1 of 3)">
    When a truck reaches **Estado 8 (Pesaje final)** in the Camiones module, the system automatically creates a new inventory record in Buques with:

    - **Tipo movimiento:** "Carga adicionada"
    - **Cliente and Producto:** Copied automatically from the truck record
    - **camion_id:** Links back to the originating truck transaction

    <Tip>The ship name (motonave) is intentionally left blank on auto-synced records. It gets assigned later when the cargo is dispatched to a ship.</Tip>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 2 — View the New Record in Buques (Step 2 of 3)">
    <Steps>
      <Step>
        Navigate to **Transporte → Buques** (`/dashboard/transporte/buques`).
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Look at the top of the inventory table for the newest entry.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Confirm the record shows **Tipo movimiento = "Carga adicionada"** and matches the truck's cliente and producto.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 3 — Verify Traceability (Step 3 of 3)">
    <Steps>
      <Step>
        Click **"Ver detalles"** on the inventory record.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        In the detail panel, locate the **camion_id** field — this UUID links back to the truck transaction for auditing purposes.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How to Filter Inventory Records

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Step 1 — Open the Filter Panel (Step 1 of 2)">
    <Steps>
      <Step>
        Navigate to the Buques inventory table.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Click the **"Filtrar"** button to open the filter panel.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 2 — Filter by Tipo Movimiento (Step 2 of 2)">
    Select **Tipo movimiento** from the filter options to isolate records by type:

    | Tipo Movimiento | Meaning |
    |---|---|
    | Carga adicionada | Auto-created when a truck reaches Estado 8 |
    | Buque despachado | Manually created when cargo is dispatched to a ship |
    | Ajuste | Adjustment or cancellation (shows negative tonnage) |
    | Carga despachada | Cargo dispatched record |

    <Tip>You can also use the **search bar** to quickly filter — for example, typing "despachado" will show only dispatch records.</Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How to Create a Manual Dispatch

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Step 1 — Open the Dispatch Form (Step 1 of 2)">
    <Steps>
      <Step>
        Navigate to **Transporte → Buques**.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Click the **"Generar carga"** button.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Note>The "Generar carga" button is only visible to **Admin** and **Operator** roles.</Note>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 2 — Complete the Dispatch Entry (Step 2 of 2)">
    Fill in the required fields for the "Buque despachado" entry, including the ship name (motonave), cantidad, and other relevant details. Submit the form to create the record.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is the difference between 'Carga adicionada' and 'Buque despachado'?">
    **Carga adicionada** records are created automatically when a truck completes weighing (Estado 8). They represent cargo added to inventory. **Buque despachado** records are created manually by operators when cargo is dispatched to a ship.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why is the ship name blank on some records?">
    Auto-synced "Carga adicionada" records do not have a ship name because the cargo has not yet been assigned to a ship. The ship name is set when an operator creates a "Buque despachado" dispatch entry.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why can't I see the 'Generar carga' button?">
    The "Generar carga" button is only available to Admin and Operator roles. If you are logged in as a Client, you can view inventory records but cannot create manual dispatch entries.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if a truck goes back from Estado 8 to Estado 7 and then to Estado 8 again?">
    A new "Carga adicionada" record will be created each time the truck reaches Estado 8. There is currently no duplicate prevention for this scenario.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do cancellations or adjustments appear?">
    Adjustments appear as "Ajuste" records with negative tonnage (e.g., -1,500 kg). This restores the corresponding amount to the inventory balance.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Important Notes

<Tip>Each auto-synced record includes a **camion_id** that traces back to the originating truck transaction — use "Ver detalles" to verify this link at any time.</Tip>

- Auto-sync happens immediately when a truck reaches Estado 8 — no manual action is required.
- The **cliente** and **producto** fields are inherited automatically from the truck record.
- Only Admin and Operator roles can create manual dispatch entries via "Generar carga."
- The inventory balance and dedicated inventory dashboard (`/dashboard/inventario`) are planned future enhancements.
